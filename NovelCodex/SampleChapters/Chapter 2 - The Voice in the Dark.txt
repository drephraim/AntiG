The darkness was not entirely complete. The crimson gem on the pedestal cast a faint, eerie glow that painted the walls in shades of blood.
Kael turned slowly. Elara’s breathing was sharp and ragged. 
"What did you do?" she demanded, her voice echoing off the stone.
"I didn't..." Kael rubbed his temples. "I just touched it. The doors shouldn't have closed."
He walked over to the heavy iron-bound doors and shoved. They didn't budge. He struck them with his shoulder, wincing at the impact. Nothing.
"We're trapped," Elara said, her voice small.
Suddenly, a voice echoed through the crypt—not from the walls, but from within their own minds.
*Seekers of the Codex. You have found the Key, but have you the wisdom to turn it?*
Kael and Elara exchanged terrified glances. The Voice of the Ancients was real.
